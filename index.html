A Letter from the Heart
❖ Open the letter ❖

Hi, Nikki—

My last email to you arrived like thunder, all lightning and broken weather. I know the words were harsh, and I never truly meant them. There were battles I was fighting that had nothing to do with you, and everything to do with trying to repair a life already cracked.

You once said, "You're a horrible person... I would, too." Oddly, I wasn't wounded. Because beneath those words, I still found care. You reminded me that even this version of myself— the one I struggle to recognize— was still worth something.

You are kind, fiercely independent, and beautifully difficult in all the right ways. The kind of woman who carries strength without asking permission.

I made choices inside a life far more complicated than either of us deserved. A home already built. A child already loved. A relationship filled with distances I could never explain. I'm sorry that our paths crossed while I was still trying to find my own.

I gave you my heart, but it was never yours to keep, nor yours to break. It always belonged to me— I simply opened its doors and invited you to live there for a little while.

You saw me without armor, without rehearsed smiles, without the careful lies people tell to appear whole.

So I cannot blame you. To do so would betray the gift of having been truly seen.

Nor can I blame you for the choices I made, or the pieces of myself I surrendered hoping you might stay.

Where I come from, love is never measured by whether it lasts. The love I gave you was never wasted, even if it ended in the language of ache.

It was my gift. No conditions. No receipts. Only hope that somewhere in your future, the kindness you received from me finds another home, even if I never could.

Thank you for letting ordinary conversations become extraordinary memories. For turning simple moments into something I still cannot explain.

I hope my love never becomes a burden you carry with guilt.

The tenderness I gave you was never a loan. You don't owe it back. Keep it. Let it remind you that once, someone loved you without asking for perfection.

You taught me things that refuse to leave my mind.

Not every woman thinks the way you do.

Not every woman can dream beyond her scars while carrying the weight of yesterday.

Not every woman fights life itself while quietly wrestling with the darkness inside her own heart.

Before I disappear, grant me one last favor.

Keep what we had somewhere gentle inside your heart. Let it become a quiet blessing for every beautiful connection that finds you next.

If my love left even the faintest imprint, don't let it be wasted.

I know that time will eventually soften us. One day, perhaps, you'll forget what happened between us.

And maybe that's how healing works.

Please keep these words between us. Don't tell my partner. I don't want my mistakes to become someone else's wounds.

If remembering me makes moving forward harder, then hate me if you must.

I'll carry that more easily than the thought of hurting the people who remain
