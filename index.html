The Truth I Left Behind Nikki,

The last words I gave you were not born from truth, but from fear.

I stood between the ruins of a home I was desperate to rebuild and the quiet place where you had once let me belong.

So I chose the cruelest language I knew.

I told you you were only a body. That I never cared. That meeting you was a mistake.

Every sentence was another stone I threw at something that had once felt sacred.

You believed them.

How could you not?

I watched the person who once spoke to me with warmth answer with flames.

You called me worthless. You wished I would disappear. You became everything my lies had forced you to become.

And I accepted it.

Because the pain belonged first to you.

But before silence becomes permanent, allow me to leave behind one truth.

I never loved you out of convenience.

I never stayed because of desire alone.

Yes, I was broken.

Yes, I made choices that shattered more than one heart.

But none of that erases what was real.

You lived inside a part of me I never showed to anyone else.

I let you witness the version of myself that existed without defenses, without rehearsed smiles, without pretending to be whole.

You never owed me your forever.

You never owed me forgiveness.

The love I gave you was never a transaction.

It was a gift.

Even if it returned to me wrapped in grief.

Thank you for proving that kindness still survives in a world filled with frightened people.

Thank you for reminding me that strength can wear softness without becoming weak.

Not every woman can dream through the weight of her own scars.

Not every woman can carry tomorrow while surviving yesterday.

You did.

And I'll remember that.

I ask only one thing before I disappear from your story.

Keep this truth between us.

Not because I deserve protection, but because too much blood has already been drawn from words.

If hatred helps you heal, then let me be the villain you remember.

If forgetting sets you free, then let time erase my name.

But somewhere, beneath every lie I left behind, know that once, I loved you honestly.

And that truth, however late, is the only thing I have left to give.
